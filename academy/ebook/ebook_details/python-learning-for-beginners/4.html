Python learning for beginners

"Learning Programming with Python" Introduction: Bangladesh's economy stands on three pillars. The first is garment industry, the second is agriculture and the third is labor of expatriate Bangladeshis. But the fourth pillar is missing. That pillar could be information technology. But due to lack of proper direction and efforts, Bangladesh is still far behind in this sector.
No matter how much we talk about information technology, no matter how much we dream of becoming rich, it will not work. The key to information technology is programming and we have to move forward with that. Although it is too late, many in Bangladesh are beginning to realize this, and so school-college students are also trying to learn programming outside of their regular studies, participating in various programming competitions. It is a matter of great joy and pride for me that most of the school-college level students in Bangladesh started learning programming with the book Computer Programming Volume 1 written by me. In the book, I have tried to introduce students to programming using C programming language.
Python is a very popular programming language for people to start learning programming in different countries of the world. Just as school-level students are starting to learn programming with Python, some of the world's best universities, such as MIT, have introduced courses to start learning programming and computer science with Python in their syllabi. This book is my small effort so that the students of Bangladesh do not fall behind. I've written a book about Python called 'Python Introduction' before, but it's not really for those who want to start learning programming, but for those who can program but want to learn Python. And this book is for those who have no idea about programming. Sixth graders and above should have no problem reading the book. The book is reviewed by Tahmid Rafi, Tanvirul Islam, Abu Ashraf Masnoon and Mafina Khan. Their review has helped me a lot to correct the various errors in the book. So they have gratitude. And my wife Sirajum Munira Parmita also gave a lot of encouragement while writing the book. I would have finished the book early to overcome his programming fears, otherwise I would have spent more time lazily.
When I met my programming guru SM Shahriar Hossain a couple of years ago, he told me something very important: The children of Bangladesh read your book, very good thing. Now if you write another book, that book should be better than the previous one. It didn't feel like I needed to write a book, I just wrote something. Or no one else writes, so I write whatever I want. ” So I tried to follow his advice.
I spent my high school life at AK High School in Dhaka. One of my favorite teachers there was Islam Sir (Science and Mathematics) and Ershad Ullah Sir (English). Although Ershad met Ullah Sir twice after school life, he did not meet Islam Sir. I thought many times that I would go to Sir's house and see him, but I could not find out exactly what I was going to do. That is why I am dedicating this book to them. Of course, this time I will be able to meet the sirs on the touch of giving the book as a gift.
Hopefully, this book will introduce millions of Bengali speaking children to the joyous world of programming. Reading the book will encourage them to learn programming. And yes, the fourth pillar of Bangladesh's economy will be built by our children one day.

Tamim Shahriar Subin
Grab R&D Center, Singapore.
May, 2017.
